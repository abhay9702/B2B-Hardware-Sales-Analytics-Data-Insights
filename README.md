### üìä **Project Motivation & Why It Was Executed**

This project was created to help **B2B Hardware**, a company that supplies computer hardware and peripherals across India, gain meaningful insights from its sales data. The **Sales Director was facing significant challenges** in understanding how the business was performing because the existing reporting was manual, inconsistent, and mostly verbal. Regional managers would verbally report quarterly figures without reliable data, which made it difficult to accurately track sales trends or understand why revenue was declining.

As sales were **declining in a dynamically growing market**, the leadership lacked a clear, data-driven view of performance, regional growth, customer patterns, or product contributions. The absence of a central tool meant that **decisions were being made based on intuition or fragmented information**, rather than real insights derived from the actual sales data. 

### üß† **Problem Statement & Company Understanding**

B2B Hardware has its head office in Delhi and several regional offices throughout India. The company struggled with:

* Lack of **automated analysis or real-time sales reporting**
* Dependency on **manual reports and verbal insights** from regional managers
* No way to **track performance trends** across regions, customers, or time periods
* Inability to make **data-backed decisions to improve sales and profitability** 

The core issue was not the absence of data ‚Äî it was the inability to **extract meaningful insights from that data efficiently**. The solution needed to enable quick analysis, uncover hidden performance gaps, and help leadership visualize trends and patterns for better decision-making.

**2Ô∏è‚É£ Profit Analysis Dashboard**

**What‚Äôs happening:**
This page focuses on profitability rather than just revenue. It highlights profit margin %, contribution %, and profit distribution across products, customers, and regions. It clearly shows cases where high revenue does not necessarily translate into high profit.

**Why it‚Äôs happening:**
Low margins can result from heavy discounting, high-cost products, inefficient pricing strategies, or regional cost differences. These issues often remain hidden when analysis is limited to revenue numbers alone.

**How the dashboard helps:**
By breaking down profit metrics visually, this dashboard helps identify low-margin customers or products that are dragging overall profitability. Stakeholders can use these insights to revise pricing, renegotiate contracts, focus on high-margin segments, or reduce dependency on unprofitable sales.

**3Ô∏è‚É£ Performance Insights Dashboard**

**What‚Äôs happening:**
This page compares top and bottom performers across regions, customers, and products. It highlights performance gaps, declining segments, and consistently strong contributors.

**Why it‚Äôs happening:**
Uneven sales execution, market maturity differences, and varying customer behavior lead to performance imbalance. Without comparison views, underperformance often goes unnoticed until revenue is impacted.

**How the dashboard helps:**
The Performance Insights page enables targeted action. Sales teams can replicate best practices from top-performing regions and intervene early in declining segments. Leadership can allocate resources more effectively based on data-backed evidence.
